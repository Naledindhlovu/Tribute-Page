Dr. Norman Borlaug

A photo of Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug was an American agronomist, humanitarian, and Nobel laureate who has been called "the father of the Green Revolution."

Borlaug was born in 1914 and grew up on a farm in Iowa. He studied plant pathology and genetics, and went on to develop high-yielding, disease-resistant varieties of wheat that helped to feed millions of people around the world.

Borlaug received numerous awards and honors for his work, including the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970. He died in 2009 at the age of 95.
